Anna Keller

PhD Candidate in Machine Learning · University of Zurich

I am a fourth-year PhD candidate in the Machine Learning & Climate Risk group at the University of Zurich, advised by Prof. Markus Vogel. My research focuses on scalable probabilistic models for climate extremes and robust decision-making under distribution shifts.

Broadly, I am interested in probabilistic ML, Bayesian deep learning, and trustworthy AI for high-stakes environmental applications.

Research

My work lies at the intersection of probabilistic modeling, deep learning and climate science. I aim to build models that provide calibrated uncertainty estimates and remain robust under distribution shifts induced by climate change.

Focus Area
Probabilistic Deep Learning

Approximate inference methods, variational objectives, and scalable Bayesian neural networks applied to spatio-temporal climate data.

Focus Area
Extreme Events

Tail modeling of heat waves and heavy precipitation using extreme value theory and generative models.

Focus Area
Decision Making

Robust optimization and uncertainty-aware policies for climate risk management and infrastructure planning.
